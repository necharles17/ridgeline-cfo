## FINDINGS COPY

Findings are shown based on score tier. Each audit has three tiers: Critical (0–39), Needs Work (40–69), Good/Excellent (70–100).

---

### FINANCIAL HEALTH CHECK, Critical (score 0–39)

1. **Cash runway is a real risk**
With less than 3 months of runway or no visibility into it, you're one slow month away from a crisis. This needs to be your first priority.

2. **No meaningful financial visibility**
Without a regular view of actuals vs. budget, you're flying blind. Even a basic monthly review would change how you make decisions.

3. **Profitability drivers are unknown**
If you don't know your margins by line, you don't know which parts of the business are worth doubling down on, and which ones are quietly dragging you down.

4. **No formal financial planning in place**
Operating without a documented budget or plan makes it nearly impossible to grow intentionally or catch problems before they compound.

### FINANCIAL HEALTH CHECK, Needs Work (score 40–69)

1. **Cash forecasting has gaps**
You have some visibility, but an inconsistent or informal forecast means surprises are more likely than they should be.

2. **Margin awareness needs sharpening**
Ballpark numbers aren't enough when pricing, hiring, or investment decisions are on the table. Precision here pays real dividends.

3. **Budget reviews are inconsistent**
Monthly reviews keep small variances from becoming big problems. Once-a-year reviews create blind spots that compound over time.

4. **Revenue trajectory needs attention**
Flat or inconsistent revenue makes planning harder and signals that growth levers may not be clearly defined or actively managed.

### FINANCIAL HEALTH CHECK, Good/Excellent (score 70–100)

1. **Strong financial foundation**
You have real visibility into your numbers, cash, margins, and profitability. That puts you ahead of most businesses at your stage.

2. **Active monitoring in place**
Regular actuals vs. budget reviews mean you catch issues early and course-correct before they compound. This discipline matters.

3. **Strategic forecasting is working**
A rolling forecast is one of the most powerful tools a growing business can have. You're using it, and that's a genuine advantage.

4. **Budget discipline is a real asset**
A formal annual budget paired with regular reviews signals financial maturity. This is the foundation for confident, data-driven growth.

---

### CFO READINESS, Critical (score 0–39)

1. **Your finance setup may be underpowered**
At your stage and complexity, relying on a bookkeeper or handling finances yourself leaves major strategic gaps. A fractional CFO could change the trajectory of your next 12 months.

2. **High-stakes decisions without financial backing**
Making major bets without solid financial models is a pattern that catches up with growing companies. The cost of one bad call often outweighs a full year of CFO fees.

3. **Capital readiness gap**
If you're considering outside capital, or already have investors, your current finance setup likely isn't built to support that. Lenders expect structured reporting and projections.

4. **Reporting exposure**
Without formal reporting infrastructure, your credibility and decision-making speed are limited. Structured financials build trust, with investors, lenders, and your own leadership team.

### CFO READINESS, Needs Work (score 40–69)

1. **You may be growing past your current setup**
A bookkeeper and outside accountant work well early on, but at $5M+ with real complexity, you need more strategic financial leadership in the room.

2. **Financial blind spots remain**
Decisions are sometimes made without full data. That works until it doesn't, and the misses at this scale tend to be expensive.

3. **Reporting structure is partial**
You have some stakeholder reporting but it's not fully formalized. As you scale, this becomes a liability, both operationally and reputationally.

4. **Moderate decision risk**
You're modeling some decisions but not consistently. Building that habit before it's urgent will save you from expensive surprises.

### CFO READINESS, Good/Excellent (score 70–100)

1. **Well-structured for your stage**
Your finance setup is aligned with where your business is, that's harder to achieve than it sounds, and it matters for what's next.

2. **Solid decision-making framework**
You're using financial data to drive major calls. That discipline separates businesses that scale cleanly from those that stumble through growth.

3. **Capital-ready posture**
Whether you're raising or not, having the financial infrastructure in place means you're not scrambling when opportunity, or pressure, hits.

4. **Reporting discipline in place**
Formal reporting to stakeholders signals maturity and builds the kind of trust that opens doors, with investors, lenders, and your own leadership team.

---

### BLIND SPOTS AUDIT, Critical (score 0–39)

1. **Pricing strategy is overdue for a review**
If you haven't formally reviewed pricing in years, or ever, you're almost certainly leaving revenue on the table. Market conditions, costs, and value perceptions shift.

2. **No visibility into CAC and LTV**
Without customer acquisition cost and lifetime value data, you can't make confident decisions about where to invest in growth. You're spending without a clear return picture.

3. **Financial controls gap**
No documented expense approvals, reconciliation, or controls means errors and leakage are likely happening undetected. This is one of the first things a CFO addresses.

4. **Books need to be caught up**
Multiple months of backlogged bookkeeping creates compounding risk, bad data, missed filings, and decisions based on numbers that no longer reflect reality.

### BLIND SPOTS AUDIT, Needs Work (score 40–69)

1. **Controls are inconsistent**
Informal controls are better than none, but they're not reliable. A documented process ensures nothing slips through as your team and transaction volume grows.

2. **Profitability data is incomplete**
A general sense of what's profitable isn't enough for confident resource allocation. You need data you can act on, not instincts you can't defend.

3. **Close process is informal**
An undocumented close process leads to inconsistent timing and data quality. This creates downstream problems for forecasting and reporting.

4. **Stress test is overdue**
If you haven't modeled a 20–30% revenue drop, you don't know what it would take to survive one. That's a risk worth understanding before you need to.

### BLIND SPOTS AUDIT, Good/Excellent (score 70–100)

1. **Proactive pricing reviews**
Regular pricing reviews signal financial maturity and ensure you're capturing fair value as your business evolves. This is a real differentiator.

2. **Unit economics are tracked and used**
Knowing your CAC and LTV, and acting on them, puts you ahead of most businesses at your stage. Growth decisions are anchored in real return data.

3. **Strong financial controls in place**
Documented and enforced controls reduce risk, prevent leakage, and make audits, due diligence, and scaling dramatically easier.

4. **Close process is buttoned up**
A consistent, documented close process gives you reliable data on a predictable timeline. That's the foundation of good financial management.
